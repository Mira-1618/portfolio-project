Let's Get to Know Each Other!

I have loved technology since I was kid: Frankensteining broken laptops with my uncle when I couldn't afford a new one, diligently trying to customize my desktop with Rainmeter despite a true and utter lack of experience, and reading my friends' CS notes between classes when I couldn't get into the course myself; I was insatiable.

Once I began attending University, it took me a couple of tries to get my major right. I thought perhaps I'd study Spanish Language, Psychology, Chemical Engineering, Pre-Law, Finance, or Political Science. Computers were a hobby, and I firmly believed that you had to be a genuine calculus-genius to thrive in the world of algorithms that seemed to come with Software Engineering. By the time I realized technology was the path for me, it would have cost a small-fortune to pivot into the school of Engineering, so I took the next best thing: Information Technology and Systems.

In my degree I took survey courses in Java, Python, R, SQL, Business Intelligence, Database Management, Cyber Security, and, notably, Technology Management. The human elements of technology really resonate with me, and as I had little natural talent for design, I went into management. I was happy with my choice for years, but the deeper I delve into people and process, the farther and farther I find myself from technology. There's little to no "hands-on keyboard" for me anymore, so in 2024 I set out to change that.

So here we are, very much in the middle of my career journey, but also at a sort of beginning! I'm thrilled and excited to be learning and re-learning what it takes to be a developer, and I hope that you'll join me on this adventure! Whether you're a fellow-student, a mentor, a cheerleader, or a potential employeer, I am thrilled and humbled that you've taken the time to read my story, and I hope you'll reach out! I'd love to be friends.

Cheers to us!
